Developer Relations

There are software companies that build products for developers. In such cases instead of a typical marketing team, the company relies on individuals with marketing and technical skills to market the product

Coding

Depending on the product you are promoting you may or may not need to code. If you are considering picking this up as a career then you will definitely have to learn some coding. You will need the skill to showcase or demo technical products.

Working in teams

You will likely be working closely with the developers to understand the software product as well as to incorporate user feedback.

Working alone

When you are out there talking to developers you will mostly be on your own, but back in your organization, you will have to work with and report to different team members.

Impact

Being able to speak the language of developers and market to them is hard to do. Making individuals who can do this very valuable.

Managing a team

If there is more than one Dev Rel person, you might end up having to manage a team.

Dealing with customers

You interact directly with customers.

Learning Curve

Learning as much coding as you can is a good start, learning to market as well as being a good communicator forms the basis of a career in Dev Rel.
