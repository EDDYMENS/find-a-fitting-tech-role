Customer Success Manager

A customer success manager ensures that customers remain happy. Their job involves reaching out to customers either passively or actively to figure out if they are ok with the product.

Since most software solutions are offered on a subscription basis it's important to reduce churn which is the number of customers canceling their subscriptions.

Customer success managers might find themselves also up-selling new products or features to existing customers.

Coding

You don't need to code to be successful in this role, but imagine you know a little bit of coding, if your product is the kind where the end-user has to deal with some level of coding, you might be able to help them out without involving other team members.

Working in teams

Customer success managers need to collaborate with different team members like sales and developers in other to do their job.

Working alone

It's hard to work solo as a customer success manager. Even if you are a consultant you will still be working within the scope of a team.

Impact

Having a healthy number of happy customers is a sign of a well-performing customer success manager.

Managing a team

Unless there are other customer success managers, you are not likely going to be managing a team.

Dealing with customers

This is literally what you do.

Learning Curve

Most of what you need to learn to succeed in this role revolves around soft skills eg: communication, sales, etc
